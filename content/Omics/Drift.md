There might be a change introduced in the data introduced over by e.g. time, temperature or by repeated runs of the instrument. Randomization makes it possible to distinguish technical variation from biological variation. When plotting the data against time a drift in the data can be revealed. 
![[Metabolomics2.pdf#page=17&rect=340,21,738,302|Metabolomics2, p.17]]The orthogonality to the treatment here implies an independence of the two trends.
Internal standards can be used to normalize the data to, but this is generally difficult in metabolomics.
Another approach is **sample pooling** and addition of quality control samples, that should have an unchanging value, to see if there is a drift over time.  ![[Metabolomics2.pdf#page=18&rect=131,19,781,265|Metabolomics2, p.18]]
