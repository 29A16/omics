Unknown metabolites pose a problem for [[2 Thoughts/Metabolomics|metabolomic analysis]] since only their degradation product might be observed. This is further complicated, when we consider the site at which we sample. Metabolomic data is most often extracted from blood samples, but metabolite profiles (and degradation products of unknown metabolites) vary drastically depending on the site where blood is drawn. ![[Metabolomics1.pdf#page=15&rect=382,30,803,442|Metabolomics1, p.15]]
Another important parameter to consider is time. If we e.g. look at Anaerobic exercise, the amount of lactate in blood varies drastically and can delay other processes like glycolysis in the liver (by inhibition of phosphofructokinas).
![[Metabolomics1.pdf#page=27&rect=391,12,779,475|Metabolomics1, p.27]]
Another influential challenge is to differentiate whether an increase of metabolite is due to an increase in flux or accumulation.

Therefore, the whole endeavour is rather complicated, since multiple organs and factors are involved, and it can be unclear, which effect is caused by which parameters.
The metabolome is the richest ome and metabolites can be hard to distinguish. There is also a big difference between organisms and weather '*xenometabolites*' are considered. ![[Metabolomics1.pdf#page=25&rect=53,14,750,278|Metabolomics1, p.25]]
