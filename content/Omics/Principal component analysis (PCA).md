PCA is an unsupervised [[Statistical analysis|statistical technique]], that takes all samples into account, instead of performing multiple tests. A new axis is drawn along the biggest difference in the data. This axis is called a principal component (PC). The multidimensionality of the data (one for each metabolite) is reduced into the principal components, that explain its variation. The first PC captures most of the variation and each subsequent PC the next highest one.
After the PCA is performed, the data points are clustered and outliers can be identified.
