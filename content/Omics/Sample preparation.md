[[Metabolomics Technology]]
Metabolic samples are very delicate and therefore require more careful preparation. Sample preparation goes through these steps:
- Quenching of all bilogical processes (either ice or liquid $\ce{N2}$)
- Reduction of sample complexity (proteins, chlorophyl,…)
- Enrichment of targets
There are only small differences in a few metabolites between serum and plasma (although when using NMR there could be a difference). 
The use of solvents on the other hand is paramount for MS analysis. Ionizing compounds, surfactants (SDS, PEG) and polymers should be avoided. Suitable solvents/salts are water, methanol, ammonium formate or acetate.

Depending on the properties of the investigated metabolites, different preparation methods have to be chosen. Polar metabolites are prepared by protein precipitation (in methanol or acetonitrile), while lipids are collected with two phase extraction (Folch — methanol, chloroform, water).
