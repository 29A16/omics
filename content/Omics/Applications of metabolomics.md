## iKnife
iKnife is a [[Aim of metabolomics|fingerprinting]] technique, that is used during surgery to assure, that the surgent is cutting outside the tumour. The knife of the surgent is collecting samples, that are processed via MS. The metabolic profile is then compared to the tumour/healthy fingerprint.
## Identification of perturbed metabolic function
**Metabolite profiling** can be used to distinguish between healthy and perturbed function of metabolic processes. E.g. comparison of $\beta$-cells for insulin production.
## Confirming the presence/absence of enzymes
**Targeted metabolomics** can be employed to confirm the presence or absence of specific enzymes in e.g. a mutant (can the final product of the metabolism be detected?)
## Biomarker discovery
This is the most common application. Efficient biomarkers can be used to identify/distinguish:
- Disease risk and outcome
- Treatment outcome
- Prevention and early detection
- Identification of benefiting patient — precision medicine
- …
If possible, the biomarker should be chosen, so that sample collection is non-invasive, inexpensive, sensitive, and specific. 
### Example: Diabetes 
The classical approach of smell and taste tests on urine includes false positives (fructose in urine), therefore biosensors that measure glucose in urine. Even better analysis can be made with an untargeted analysis of urine/blood. The more complete picture can be used to identify other relevant biomarkers, that might even have an influence on the best choice of treatment (precision medicine).
## Biomarker for multiple diseases
Although the metabolome is very dynamic over multiple parameters (sex, age, time, lifestyle), there are some metabolites, that are shared across different diseases.
