To minimize [[Errors in Metabolomics|errors]] in (metabolomic) experiments, the **experimental planning phase** is very important. Optimally there should be as view experiments as possible, that can be done in the timespan of one day by one person.
The experiment itself should follow a **factorial design**, to optimize the amount of experiment runs and allow for adequate randomization. For this all relevant variables have to be considered, and **confounding effects** (affect the studied relationship without being the actual cause of variance) have to be either blocked or removed with randomization.
For this the whole workflow has to be considered, and confounding effects might be found at unlikely points — see below, where the hospital at which the measurement was conducted represents a confounding variable.
