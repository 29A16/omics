When considering randomization for the [[Experimental setup and confounding effects|experimental setup]], there is a decision to be made between **randomization** and **constrained randomization**. While normal randomization tries to remove confounding effects of experimental variation unpacifically, constrained randomization tries to block specific confounding effects, by creating randomization within groups.
All groups contain the factors relevant for the experiment, while randomizing all other conditions/factors. This allows the relevant factors to be controlled over other confounding factors (time, temperature …). When e.g. investigating the effect of gastric bypass surgery on diabetes, each group should contain patients pre- and post-surgery.
