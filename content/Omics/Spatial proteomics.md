[[Spatial omics]]
## Immunofluorescence
A primary Antibody (Ab) is used to identify the target in the tissue sample, that undergoes different steps of identification (with secondary Ab/direct coupling with fluorophore/oligonucleotide tag) followed by a bleaching step and identification with another method (different target). This process is called **cyclic immunofluorescence** and is used to increase the amount of proteins that can be stained in the same sample. Around 30 different markers can be used.

## Targeted MS
This technique also uses Ab to identify the protein target, but they are conjugated with metal isotopes. Either lasers or ion beams are used to release the isotopes locally, followed by an analysis in MS. This process destroys the tissue. The images have a subcellular resolution and a maximum of 40 markers can be used, they are created by overlaying multiple signals of different markers.
