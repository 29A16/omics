There are quite a few errors to look out for when conducting metabolomic experiments. 
**Selection bias** can occur on a biological level (genetic) and temporal (Temperature, collection site) as discussed before ([[Metabolomics problems]]).
Some things can also go wrong with the **sample preparation**, relating to the preparation technique itself and storage conditions.
**Analytical biases** can be introduced due to the sample complexity or analytical setup (different ionization of species in MS)
Last but not least, there can be **interpretive biases** with the annotation of metabolite and the statistical assumptions (normal vs. log distribution).
There can also be some errors made by the researcher, like inadequate hypothesis.

## Circumvent biases
Some errors can be avoided by:
- Considering difference in working techniques between personal
- Clear protocols can minimize some variation
- Considering the batch effect in the analysis
- Controlling/considering environmental factors, such as date/time, temperature in, and the location of the laboratory
