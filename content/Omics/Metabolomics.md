#startingpoint 
Metabolotes are the final product of organisms following the dogma of molecular biology. The regulation of biological processes follows complex networks, where metabolites play an active role.
>[!cite] Definition
>*The Metabolome is the complete set of small molecule chemicals found within a biological sample* 

Therefore, it is technically incorrect to refer to metabolomics (which would be the comprehensive analysis and identification of compounds) and *metabonomics* is a more accurate description when not analysing all metabolites of a sample.
## Regulation of metabolism
Depending on the needs of the organism, different pathways are regulated up or down by different means:
- Modification of the **amount of enzyme** 
	- Transcription/translation - slow
	- RNA/protein degradation
	- Secretion from compartments - fast
- **Catalytic activity**
	- Allosteric or covalent modification (phosphorylation o=r co substrate)
	- Association with regulatory proteins
- **Availability of substrate/product level**
	- Regulation at entrance and exit of the pathway (if product isn't needed, the substrate isn't taken up)
Another aspects of the metabolism is its **intermediaty**, which describes its fine fragmentation to make the processes more efficient and complete. More intermediates enable the production of all necessary compounds.
**Compartmentalization** of the metabolism into different organoids also plays an important role in setting optimal reaction conditions. This brings some oportunities but also makes metabolomic analysis more complicated in other regards:
- Metabolites are produced in different compartments complicating the analysis of a specific pathway
- Isolation of singular organoid is possible but difficult, since the equilibrium of metabolites is affected by its surroundings.
