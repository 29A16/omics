#startingpoint 
Epigenetic modifications introduce stable changes in genetic expression, without introducing changes of the DNA sequence itself. The most prominent example is the (heritable) methylation of Cytosine by DNMTs.![[Immunotek_Louella_presentation.pptx.pdf#page=4&rect=5,105,412,250|Immunotek_Louella_presentation.pptx, p.4]]
Another mode of epigenetic alteration are numerous histone modifications, that make the DNA more or less accessible for transcription.
